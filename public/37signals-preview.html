Every decision in life, you make out of love or out of fear.

A yoga teacher said that to me years ago, and it rewired everything. Not gradually. Immediately. I started seeing it everywhere: in the way I ran my companies, in the way I treated my team, in the way I showed up at home. Most of what I thought were rational business decisions were actually fear dressed up in a spreadsheet.

I've been building companies for over 25 years. I started as a teenager in Germany, painfully shy, running a business I was barely brave enough to talk about. I've never had a real job. What I've had instead is a long, messy education in figuring out what actually matters.

The turning point wasn't a business win. It was my wife's breast cancer diagnosis. She's fine today. But lying awake at 3am, I realized something that changed the trajectory of everything: the biggest risk isn't failing at something new. The biggest risk is succeeding at something that doesn't matter.

We sold the company. I rebuilt from scratch. Not because the business was broken, but because I was building the wrong thing.

Now I run a portfolio of companies with over 500 employees. But the thing I care most about is Love Not Fear, an operating system for living and leading with purpose. Four pillars: find your purpose, define your values, craft your vision, build your habits. We use it with students trying to figure out what to do with their lives, entrepreneurs who've lost the plot, and companies that want to hire and lead differently.

I'm also writing a book about all of this. It's not a self-help book. It's a self-leadership book. There's a difference.

What I believe

Most personal development is backwards. People set goals, build habits, and grind, then wonder why they feel empty when they hit the number. That's because they skipped the foundation. You can't build a system for your life if you don't know what your life is for.

Purpose comes first. Not passion. Purpose. Passion is what you enjoy. Purpose is what you'd do even when it's hard. Once you know that, values become your filter. Vision becomes your direction. Habits become your engine. In that order.

I believe most fear-based decisions look rational on the surface. Staying in a job you've outgrown because "the market is tough." Not raising prices because "customers might leave." Not having the hard conversation because "the timing isn't right." These feel like logic. They're not. They're fear wearing a reasonable mask.

I believe you should build companies that make the world slightly less broken, not slightly more optimized. I believe the best leaders are the ones who've done the inner work. I believe you can be ambitious and kind at the same time, and that anyone who tells you otherwise is protecting their own cynicism.

I believe simplicity is a discipline, not a style. The hardest thing in business, and in life, is saying no to good things so you can say yes to the right things.

Values

1. First Things First. Always prioritize health and your loved ones. If it's not a "FUCK YES," it's a "NO."

2. Own It. Always do your best. Be impeccable with your word. Do not complain or blame. Find solutions.

3. Create Impact. Enrich the lives of everybody we come in touch with. Don't take things personal. Do not make assumptions.

4. Unconditional Love. Make decisions out of love, not fear.

5. Simplicity. Be Authentic. Only do and buy things that matter.

The book

I'm writing a book on self-leadership. Not the "10 habits of successful people" kind. The kind where I tell you about the time I was so shy I couldn't make a phone call to my own customers, and how a yoga teacher's one sentence changed everything, and what happened when my wife got cancer and I had to decide what my life was actually for.

It's half book, half workbook. Dense, no fluff. Every chapter has a Beliefs section (what to think about) and a Behaviors section (what to do about it). The working title is Love Not Fear.
